# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We pledge to make participation in the C-Address Onboarding Bridge project a respectful, harassment-free experience for everyone. We welcome contributors regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that helps create a positive community include:

- Showing empathy and respect toward other people
- Giving and receiving constructive feedback with care
- Accepting responsibility, apologizing when needed, and learning from mistakes
- Focusing on what is best for the project and its users
- Being patient with newcomers and people using different communication styles

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Sexualized language or imagery, and unwelcome sexual attention
- Insults, trolling, personal attacks, or repeated disruptive comments
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing another person's private information without explicit permission
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable behavior. They may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, pull requests, discussions, and other contributions that do not follow this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers should apply this Code of Conduct consistently and fairly. Maintainers who do not follow or enforce it in good faith may face temporary or permanent consequences from other project leadership.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including the repository, issue tracker, pull requests, discussions, community chat, release processes, and project events. It also applies when someone represents the project in public spaces, such as using an official project email address, social media account, or event role.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the project maintainers by opening a private security advisory or contacting the maintainers through the repository's published security contact path. Reports should include as much context as is safely possible.

Maintainers will review reports promptly and respectfully. They will protect reporter privacy and limit shared details to what is necessary for investigation and response.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers may use the following impact-based guidelines when deciding consequences.

### 1. Correction

Community impact: A single inappropriate comment or action that appears unintentional or low impact.

Consequence: A private or public correction explaining why the behavior was inappropriate. The maintainer may request an apology or edit.

### 2. Warning

Community impact: A violation through one incident or a pattern of minor incidents.

Consequence: A warning describing the violation and expected behavior. Continued violations may lead to temporary restrictions.

### 3. Temporary Ban

Community impact: Serious violation of community standards, sustained inappropriate behavior, or repeated violations after warning.

Consequence: Temporary loss of access to project communication or contribution spaces. The person should not interact with affected community members during this period.

### 4. Permanent Ban

Community impact: A pattern of violations, harassment, threats, or conduct that makes continued participation unsafe or disruptive.

Consequence: Permanent removal from project spaces and contribution channels.
